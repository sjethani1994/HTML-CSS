Featured Books

Book Name: Godan Munshi Premchand

Author: J Rantan and P.Lal
"Godan" by Munshi Premchand is a poignant portrayal of a peasant named Hori and his unfulfilled desire to own a cow, representing the struggles of marginalized farmers in pre-independent India, exposing the complexities of societal structures and the challenges faced by the rural poor.

Book Name: HangWomen

Author: K.R. Meera
The story revolves around the life of a young woman named Chetna Grddha Mullick, who becomes the first female executioner in India, exploring themes of gender, power, and societal norms through a compelling narrative that delves into the complexities of human existence.

Book Name: Midnight children

Author: Salman Rushdie
The novel follows the life of Saleem Sinai, born at the stroke of midnight on the day of India's independence, and the intertwined destinies of children born at the same time, reflecting the tumultuous journey of India post-independence.

Book Name: Great Indian Novel

Author: Shashi Tharoor
"The Great Indian Novel" ingeniously weaves Indian mythology, particularly the Mahabharata, with modern political events, presenting a witty and critical account of post-independence India's societal and political evolution

Book Name: No Name Lane

Author: Howard Linskey
A gripping psychological thriller that follows the mysterious events in No Name Lane, uncovering dark secrets and sinister truths that shake the foundations of a small, seemingly tranquil town.

Book Name: Raavan: Enemy of Aryavarta

Author: Amish Tripathi
"Raavan: Enemy of Aryavarta" delves into the life and journey of the legendary antagonist from Indian mythology, portraying his complex persona and the events that led to his transformation into one of the most intriguing characters in the epic of Ramayana.
